Key moments in the life of a true legend:

Birth and Childhood (1978–1996)

Kobe Bryant was born on August 23, 1978, in Philadelphia. As the son of former NBA player Joe Bryant, he spent part of his childhood in Italy, where his father continued his professional career. After returning to the U.S., he attended Lower Merion High School, where he earned national recognition as the top high school basketball player.

Entering the NBA (1996)

In 1996, directly after graduating high school, Bryant was selected 13th overall in the NBA draft by the Charlotte Hornets and was immediately traded to the Los Angeles Lakers. In his rookie season, he won the NBA Slam Dunk Contest and quickly gained a reputation as a talented young player.

Lakers Dynasty and Championships (1999–2002)

Alongside Shaquille O'Neal, Bryant led the Lakers to three consecutive NBA championships in 2000, 2001, and 2002. Their on-court chemistry was key to the team’s dominance during that time.

Controversy and Career Development (2003–2007)

In 2003, Bryant was accused of sexual assault; the charges were later dropped, and the case was settled out of court. During this period, after O'Neal left the Lakers in 2004, Bryant became the undisputed leader of the team. In 2006, he scored 81 points in a single game—the second-highest in NBA history.

Return to the Top and More Championships (2008–2010)

Bryant won the NBA regular season MVP in 2008. He led the Lakers to three straight NBA Finals appearances, winning championships in 2009 and 2010, and earned Finals MVP honors both years.

Later Career and Injuries (2010–2016)

In 2012, Bryant won his second Olympic gold medal with Team USA. In 2013, he suffered a serious Achilles tendon injury that affected the remainder of his career. After several injuries, he announced his retirement following the 2015–2016 season. In his final game on April 13, 2016, he scored 60 points against the Utah Jazz.

Post-Career Activities (2016–2020)

After retiring, Bryant became involved in media and educational projects. In 2018, he won an Oscar for the animated short film Dear Basketball.

Tragic Death (2020)

On January 26, 2020, Bryant died in a helicopter crash in Calabasas, California, along with his 13-year-old daughter Gianna and seven others. His death was met with widespread mourning around the world.

Legacy and Commemoration

In 2020, Bryant was posthumously inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame. In 2021, he was honored as a member of the NBA 75th Anniversary Team. In 2025, he was again inducted into the Hall of Fame as a member of the 2008 Olympic team. In Orange County, August 24 has been established as Kobe Bryant Day.
